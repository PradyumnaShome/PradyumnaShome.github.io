About

I am a researcher and educator specializing in human-centered design and sustainable technology. My work explores the intersection of natural interfaces and digital systems, focusing on creating technology that enhances rather than overwhelms human experience.

Currently, I lead the Organic Computing Lab at the University of Example, where our team investigates novel interaction paradigms inspired by natural systems. Our recent work has been recognized with awards from ACM CHI and featured in leading HCI venues.

Research Interests

My research spans several interconnected areas: designing calm technology that respects human attention, developing sustainable computing practices, and exploring biomimetic approaches to interface design. I'm particularly interested in how we can learn from natural ecosystems to create more resilient and harmonious digital environments.
