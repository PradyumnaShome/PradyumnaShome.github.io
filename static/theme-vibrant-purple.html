About Me

I'm a Researcher at Carnegie Mellon University within the CyLab Security and Privacy Institute and Human-Computer Interaction Institute. I hold an MS in Computer Science from the Georgia Institute of Technology, where my work explored microarchitectural side-channel attacks and usable security.

I'm passionate about building privacy-preserving systems that are accessible to both developers and end-users, leveraging applied cryptography and confidential computing. Recently, I've been investigating privacy challenges in generative AI chatbots.

🎓

Security Seminar

Founded the Georgia Tech Security Seminar, a vibrant forum where students and faculty engage with cutting-edge security and privacy research from leading academic conferences.

💼

Industry Experience

Previously a Software Engineer on Messenger Growth at Meta in the San Francisco Bay Area, working on large-scale systems and user growth.

🔬

Research Interests

Microarchitectural side-channels, confidential computing, usable security, and privacy in AI systems. Focused on making complex security technology accessible to everyone.

I earned a BS in Computer Science from the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, advised by Chris Fletcher.
