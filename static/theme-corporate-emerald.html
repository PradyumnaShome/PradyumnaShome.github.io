About

I am a computational researcher with over two decades of experience in distributed systems, algorithmic optimization, and large-scale data analysis. My work has fundamentally shaped how modern computing infrastructure handles complexity at scale.

As the founding director of the Center for Computational Excellence, I have built a world-class research program that bridges theoretical computer science with practical applications in finance, healthcare, and national security. Our innovations have been adopted by Fortune 500 companies and government agencies worldwide.

Academic Leadership

Beyond research, I serve on the editorial boards of three premier journals, chair the ACM Special Interest Group on Scalable Computing, and advise national policy on technology infrastructure. My commitment to excellence in both scholarship and service has been recognized with the IEEE Distinguished Contribution Award and election to the National Academy of Engineering.
