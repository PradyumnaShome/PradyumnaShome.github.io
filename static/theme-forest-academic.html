About

I am a scholar of ancient texts with particular expertise in comparative mythology, classical rhetoric, and the transmission of philosophical traditions across cultures. My work seeks to illuminate the enduring relevance of ancient wisdom for contemporary intellectual discourse.

Over three decades of scholarship, I have published extensively on the relationship between Greek, Roman, and Near Eastern literary traditions. My monograph "Echoes of Antiquity" received the prestigious Bancroft Prize and has been translated into twelve languages. I currently hold the Weatherby Chair in Classical Studies and serve as Master of Thornton College.

Research Methodology

My approach combines rigorous philological analysis with broader cultural and historical contextualization. I believe that understanding ancient texts requires not merely linguistic competence but also deep engagement with the material culture, social structures, and philosophical frameworks of antiquity. This integrative methodology has proven essential for recovering nuances often lost in traditional scholarship.

Current Research

Manuscript Studies

Critical edition of newly discovered fragments from the Library of Alexandria, revealing previously unknown commentaries on Aristotelian logic.

Rhetorical Theory

Comparative analysis of persuasive techniques in classical oratory and their influence on medieval and Renaissance humanism.

Cultural Exchange

Tracing the dissemination of Hellenistic philosophical concepts through Silk Road trade networks and their integration into Asian thought.
