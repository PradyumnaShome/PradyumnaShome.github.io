About

I am a researcher specializing in human-computer interaction, with a focus on creating intuitive and accessible digital experiences. My work combines rigorous empirical methods with creative design thinking to solve complex interaction challenges.

My research sits at the intersection of cognitive science, design, and engineering. I lead a diverse team exploring novel interaction paradigms, from augmented reality interfaces to adaptive AI systems that learn from user behavior. Our work has been recognized with multiple best paper awards and has influenced the design of products used by millions.

Research Philosophy

I believe that technology should amplify human capabilities without overwhelming us. My approach emphasizes iterative co-design with users, robust empirical validation, and a commitment to creating systems that are not only powerful but also delightful and accessible to diverse populations. Every project begins with understanding real human needs and ends with measurable impact.
